Colors

In the visual arts, Color Theory is the body of practical guidance for color mixing and the visual effects of a specific color combination. Color terminology based on the color wheel and its geometry separates colors into primary color, secondary color, and tertiary color (Click the Image for further info). Color, as perceived by people, consists of 3 elements every Artist should be able to change on the flow:


Hue Hue refers to the pure spectrum colors, such as red, blue, and yellow. It represents the basic color names we use to identify different hues
Saturation Also known as chroma or intensity, saturation refers to the vividness or richness of color. A highly saturated color appears vibrant and intense, while a desaturated color appears more muted or grayish
Value Value refers to the brightness or darkness of a color. It is determined by how much black or white is mixed with the pure hue. Higher values result in lighter tints, while lower values create darker shades

Brushes

Brushes in digital art are essential tools used by artists to create various textures, effects, and styles in their digital artwork. They offer a wide range of possibilities, allowing artists to mimic traditional media like oil, watercolor, charcoal, and more. Digital brushes come in diverse forms:

    These are the foundation brushes for digital art, including round brushes for sketching and flat brushes for solid fills. They serve as the starting point for most artworks.

    These brushes replicate different textures, such as wood, paper, or fabric, adding depth and realism to digital paintings. They are useful for creating tactile and visually appealing surfaces.

    Special effects brushes enable artists to add unique elements like stars, smoke, fire, or lightning to their compositions. These brushes add a touch of magic and fantasy to the artwork.

    Designed to mimic traditional ink pens, inking brushes are precise and often used for outlining, comic book art, or fine linework.

    Popular for lettering and typography, calligraphy brushes offer a variety of styles for creating beautiful and decorative text in artworks.

    Many artists create their own custom brushes, tailored to their specific needs. These can range from complex pattern brushes to brushes that mimic unique strokes or techniques.
